How Co-ops Work

A co-op is a business voluntarily owned and controlled by the people who use it – its owners. Members of the community can be partial owners and benefit from the company’s profits, receive discounts and have access to higher quality products. Adhering to democratic principles, co-ops provide millions of jobs, stimulate the economy, and enhance the communities that they’re a part of.
